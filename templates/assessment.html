How scoring works

A transparent, straightforward scoring method. No hidden formulas.

1 – 5

Each response has a score

Every question has five options, scored from 1 (earliest stage) to 5 (most advanced). You pick the one that best matches your institution right now.

x̅

Pillar score = average

Each pillar has 5 questions. Your pillar score is the average of those 5 responses. A pillar score of 3.4 means you averaged 3.4 across its five questions.

∑ ÷ 5

Overall = average of pillars

Your overall readiness score is the average of all five pillar scores. That overall number maps to one of the four developmental levels.

For example: if your pillar scores are 2.4, 3.0, 1.8, 2.6, and 3.2, your overall score is 2.6 (Building). Each pillar weighs equally. There are no penalties or bonuses. See the developmental levels below for what each score range means.

Understanding your results

Your responses map to one of four developmental levels. These levels describe where an institution is in the process, from early conversations to deep integration. They reflect progress, not performance.

Exploring

1.0 – 2.0

Aware of AI's potential. Early conversations are happening. No formal strategy yet, but curiosity and interest are growing.

Building

2.1 – 3.0

Pilot initiatives underway. Policies are being drafted. Some faculty are adopting AI tools. A foundation is forming.

Advancing

3.1 – 4.0

Coordinated AI strategy in place. Cross-departmental adoption. Dedicated resources and growing institutional support.

Leading

4.1 – 5.0

AI is deeply embedded in institutional operations. Contributing to the broader field. Mentoring peer institutions.
